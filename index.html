About Me.

Just a few things I've done and a few things I'm doing

In product and growth analytics, I am most often interested in how people engage with products and their marketing campaigns. I'm particularly interested in understanding signals and predictors that drive product engagement which can then be used to inform product decisions and better understand the user. I use a variety of analytical techniques like machine learning, computational models, and statistical analyses (most of which I'm classically trained in, but for the most part I like exploring and learning new techniques)

I am also an adjunct professor in Business Analytics and Information Systems in the School of Management at the University of San Francisco. I teach both undergraduates and MBAs analytical techniques to better facilitate data-driven decisions -- hopefully applying these techniques in industry.
